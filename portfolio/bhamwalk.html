Draw your walk to school! Dibuja tu camino a la escuela!

This poster-sized map is intended to be an interactive learning tool for kids navigating their neighborhood walk to school. It is designed to be drawn on repeatedly using dry erase markers. Students can find the street they live on and using sidewalks, trails, crosswalks, and traffic signals, draw their route to school. Every piece of the map was in consideration of the audience:

  • Spanish and English are in equal visual hierarchy for a classroom with different home languages.
  • The colors are based on the shapes seen below the school (Sunnyland) but are actually in the windows of the school.
  • The text is in comic sans because it is an accessible typeface for learning readers and learners with dyslexia.
  • Walk area radii give an approximate walk time.
  • Recognizable landmarks and labeled streets assist students spatial reasoning.
